# Pi as Stable Perception

Pi is often introduced as a decimal:

```text
3.14159265...
```

But this is not the best perceptual entry point.

A learner first needs to experience that every circle preserves a stable relation between its radius and its boundary.

## The perceptual idea

A circle can grow or shrink. The absolute length changes. But the turning relation remains stable.

This stability is the beginning of pi.

Pi is not first a mysterious decimal. It is the invariant that appears when circular turning remains balanced.

## A teaching path

1. Show several circles.
2. Mark the radius.
3. Use string to compare the boundary against the radius.
4. Notice that the same kind of relation returns.
5. Only then introduce the formula.

The decimal expansion becomes secondary. The perceptual invariant comes first.
