# Why This Book Exists

This book begins with a child and a geometry problem.

A child can know a theorem and still not see where it applies. He can copy a proof and still not experience why the proof had to be true. This gap is not simply a lack of memory or intelligence. It is often a failure of perceptual organization.

The question that started Intention Space was therefore not:

> how can mathematics be solved by a machine?

It was:

> how can perception be managed so that mathematical understanding becomes reachable?

This question slowly expanded into software, artificial intelligence, layout, music, orchestration, and computation. But the original concern remains: how does understanding become visible?

## A practical stance

The book does not begin by claiming a new mathematics. It begins by observing a real difficulty in learning.

A learner needs more than information. A learner needs help forming the right field of attention. The relevant relation must become visible. The unnecessary detail must move away. The object of thought must stabilize.

Intention Space is proposed as a framework for supporting this process.
