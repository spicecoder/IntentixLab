# The Stance of the Book

This book is written from experience rather than from abstract authority.

It does not speak as a finished doctrine. It speaks as an investigation that began in teaching, moved through software, and reached the question of AI.

The central stance is:

> intelligence is not only the manipulation of symbols; it also requires the stabilization of perception.

This does not reject mathematics. It respects mathematics deeply. But it asks how a learner arrives at the point where a mathematical relation becomes visible.

## What the book avoids

The book avoids three traps:

1. claiming that Intention Space replaces mathematics
2. claiming that AI is useless because it lacks human perception
3. hiding practical educational problems behind technical language

## What the book offers

The book offers a language for discussing perception, relevance, identity, and action:

- Pulses as perceptual units
- Fields as evolving situational realities
- Intentions as direction of engagement
- CPUX as orchestration of understanding and execution
- CGP and PCS as coordinate languages for perceptual placement

The reader should feel that these ideas came from a real problem, not from a desire to invent terminology.
