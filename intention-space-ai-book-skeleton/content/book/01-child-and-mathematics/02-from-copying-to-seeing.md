# From Copying to Seeing

A copied proof can be correct and still not be understood.

Understanding begins when the learner can say:

> now I see why that step matters.

This moment is not just emotional. It marks a reorganization of perception. What was previously a collection of marks becomes a structured situation.

## The copied proof

In a copied proof, the student may follow the sequence:

1. statement
2. reason
3. next statement
4. next reason

But the sequence can remain external.

## The seen proof

In a seen proof, the student perceives a pressure toward resolution. The diagram becomes active. The theorem is no longer a memorized item; it becomes the right response to the situation.

Intention Space treats this transition as a movement from disconnected pulses toward field coherence.
