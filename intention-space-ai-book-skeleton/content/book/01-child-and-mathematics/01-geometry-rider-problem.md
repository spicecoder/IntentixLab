# The Geometry Rider Problem

A geometry rider is a special kind of challenge. The student is not merely asked to calculate. The student must see a hidden relation.

Two students may know the same theorem. One sees the proof almost immediately. The other stares at the diagram and sees only lines and labels.

This difference is not fully explained by logic. The proof is logical, but the arrival at the proof is often perceptual.

## What the student must perceive

A rider may require the student to notice:

- a hidden equal angle
- a parallel relation
- a symmetry
- a conserved length
- a triangle that should be compared with another triangle
- a construction that makes a relation visible

Until the relation is perceived, the theorem remains inert.

## The key educational problem

The problem is not simply:

> does the student know the theorem?

The deeper problem is:

> can the student form the perceptual field in which the theorem becomes relevant?

This is the original educational ground of Intention Space.
