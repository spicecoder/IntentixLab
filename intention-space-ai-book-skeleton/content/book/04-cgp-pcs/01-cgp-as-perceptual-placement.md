# CGP as Perceptual Placement

Canonical Grid Positioning begins with a simple shift:

> define the space first, then place the Pulse inside that space.

Instead of asking a human to think in pixels, CGP lets the human think in bounded regions.

## Why this matters

Responsive layout is not only a technical problem. It is a perceptual decision.

The same content may need a different alignment on desktop and mobile. A human designer decides what alignment preserves meaning. CGP gives that decision a common language.

## CGP as a bridge

CGP bridges:

- human spatial intention
- machine pixel projection
- device-independent layout
- Pulse-level placement

This is why CGP is central to GridLookout.
