# Intention Space and AI — Book Workspace

This book begins from a simple human problem:

> how can a child be helped to genuinely see mathematics rather than merely repeat procedures?

Intention Space did not begin as an attempt to replace mathematics or artificial intelligence. It began from the frustration of teaching: a child can look at a geometry rider, know many theorems, and still not perceive the relation that makes the proof possible.

The central claim of this book is modest but deep:

> understanding depends on managed perception.

Mathematics, software, AI, music, design, and learning all require some form of perceptual stabilization. Intention Space introduces Pulses, Fields, Intentions, GridLookout, CGP, PCS, and CPUX as practical tools for making that stabilization visible and actionable.

## Working stance

This book does not claim that current mathematics is wrong. It asks whether the human path into mathematics begins earlier than formal symbols: in perception, attention, placement, relation, and resolution.
