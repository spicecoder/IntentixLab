# Pulse, Field, and Identity

In ordinary software, identity is usually assigned first.

An object has an ID. A record has a key. A variable has a name.

In perception, identity often emerges more gradually.

A child sees many marks on a page. Slowly, a triangle becomes the relevant triangle. A circle becomes the relevant circle. A pair of angles becomes the relevant relation.

## Identity as stabilization

Intention Space treats identity as something that can emerge from stable pulse relations.

A mathematical object becomes meaningful when enough related pulses hold together:

- boundary recognized
- relation preserved
- role in the problem clarified
- action possibility opened

This is not a rejection of formal identity. It is a way to describe how the learner arrives at identity.
