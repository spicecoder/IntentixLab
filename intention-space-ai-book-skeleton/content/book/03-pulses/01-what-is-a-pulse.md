# What Is a Pulse?

A Pulse is a named perceptual concern.

It is not just data. It is not just a visual element. It is a small unit of attention that can participate in understanding and action.

A Pulse may say:

```text
parallel_lines_seen = Y
angle_relation_unclear = UN
circle_boundary_traced = Y
```

The value may be yes, no, or unresolved. This trivalent state is important because learning often lives in the unresolved middle.

## Why Pulses matter

A learner does not understand a proof all at once. Understanding emerges through small stabilizations:

- this line is parallel to that line
- this angle matches that angle
- this construction creates a useful triangle
- this theorem now becomes relevant

Each of these may be treated as a Pulse.

## Pulse as a perceptual handle

The purpose of a Pulse is to give perception a handle. Once a concern is named, it can be placed, tracked, tested, expanded, and connected.
